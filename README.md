# FE5211 — 95% CVaR for a Multi-Asset Portfolio (3-Year Horizon)

**Question:** What is the 95% Conditional Value-at-Risk of a 60/40+alts portfolio over a three-year horizon?

**Headline answer:** **18.73%** (cumulative log loss).

The naive Gaussian closed-form answer is 8.81%; we double it. Three pathologies of the data drive the gap, and each is addressed in turn.

## 5. Story (mirrors `methodology.md` and the slide deck)

1. **Data and problem.** Standard 60/40+alts portfolio (SPY 40%, AGG 20%, PE 25%, NPI 15%) with annualized expected returns 6 / 4.5 / 9.5 / 7%. The naive answer is 8.81%, but three pathologies make it wrong.
2. **Modeling approach.** Architecture: unsmooth PE/NPI (Kalman filter chosen over FGW and OW) → fit per-asset marginals (EWMA + Skew-t for SPY/AGG; semi-parametric for PE/NPI) → fit Student-t copula (ν = 4.443; the R-Vine alternative is rejected by Vuong, p = 0.0085).
3. **Model comparison.** A 4 × 2 ES matrix shows that Filtered HS and Champion are the two credible answers on unsmoothed data; FHS gives 33.05% (in-sample heavy) while the model-based Champion gives 18.73%.
4. **Decomposition.** Per-asset Component CVaR (Euler additive) reveals that SPY dominates risk (87% of total) and AGG is a small effective hedge (-12%) — capital weights and risk weights diverge sharply.
5. **Validation.** Three layers: 1Q backtest passes Kupiec but not Christoffersen (single-regime model, an expected limitation); historical replay places the GFC at the 96.4 percentile and the worst 3-year window at 98.8; stress and reverse stress quantify how far events would have to push to double the ES.
6. **Conclusion.** ES = **18.73%** with point CIs [18.59, 18.87] (MC noise) and parameter range [15.0, 22.5] (Wald). The Tier B / Tier A ratio is ~27 — parameter uncertainty dominates by an order of magnitude.

## 6. Reproducibility

- **Random seed:** `SEED = 42` everywhere (master seed in `src/config.py`); sub-modules use deterministic offsets so they can be re-run independently.
- **Caching:** Each stage writes to `cache/` so re-runs of downstream stages skip refits. Use `python main.py --force` to invalidate the cache.
- **Sanity gate:** `tests/test_numbers.py` asserts every key number in `numbers.json` against canonical values with tiered tolerances (1e-3 for parameters, 0.30 pp for MC ES values, 0.50 pp for bootstrap CI bounds).
